POSITIONS

These are the different roles a player might have on the pitch and also the corresponding area on the pitch that they might therefore occupy in order to perform their role effectively. Hence it relates to their physical position on the pitch. The 3 types of position

Defence

  • Usually 2-4 players + a goalie
  • Duty is to prevent the ball going in to the goal they are defending
  • Will be the ones to tak "16s" which is when the ball is taken from the top of the D after the attacking team hits it off the backline
  • 4 defenders will stand in the goal in a short corner (which I will explain more about later)

Midfield

  • As the name suggests these players stand in the middle of the pitch
  • Is the posittion that requires the most running
  • Role is to transfer the ball up the pitch towards the goal we want to score into (from the defenders to the attackers)
  • Their central position means they are in an ideal position to support both attackers and defender when required

Attack (Forward)

  • Role is to score goals
  • Player must be physically within the D in order to be able to score a goal



















Goalie

Although goalkeepers are technically part of the defence I thought it would be more appropriate to give them their own seperate category given that the way they play so diffferent to the other players and different rules apply to them.




Basic Rules to get started

  • Ball most only be touched by the stick (unless you are a goalie)
  • Only the front side fo the stick can hit the ball. The curved side is called the backstick and is not allowed to touch the ball ever.
  • 11 players on the pitch
















Essential Equipment

  • Hockey stick
  • Shinpads (and long socks)
  • Gum guard
  • And clothes you can comfortably move around in /run in
  • In a formal game you might also be required to wear certain colour socks and t-shirt with a number on the back to distinguish yourself as part of a team.
